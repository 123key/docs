### Definition

A subordinating conjunction introduces a subordinate clause, i.e., a clause which is dependent on a superordinate clause. A list of subordinate conjunctions can be found in Smyth 1920: <a href="http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Smyth+grammar+2770&fromdoc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.04.0007" target="_blank">631</a> (and references therein).

#### Examples

* <b>_ὅτι_</b> "that"
* <b>_εἰ_</b> "if"
* <b>_ὅτε_</b> "when"
